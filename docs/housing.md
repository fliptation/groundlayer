# Layer 7: Housing & Shelter

## The Problem

Housing has become a speculative asset class. In cities worldwide, homes sit empty as investments while people sleep on streets. Construction is dominated by a few corporations using proprietary methods. Rents consume half of many people's income. The connection between shelter and community has been severed.

Land prices are driven up by speculators who never intend to live on the land. Building codes, while necessary for safety, have been co-opted to protect industry monopolies and prevent affordable alternatives. The result: a planet with more empty homes than homeless people.

## The Alternative

A housing system designed around dignity, affordability, and community.

### Community Land Trusts for Housing

Land is held in trust so homes remain permanently affordable. Residents own their buildings but not the land beneath, preventing speculation and keeping housing within reach of future generations.

**How it works:**
- A community trust acquires land and holds it permanently
- Homeowners buy the structure but lease the land at fair rates
- Resale prices are capped to maintain affordability
- The land can never be sold to speculators

### Co-Housing & Cooperative Living

Intentional communities where residents share common spaces — kitchens, workshops, gardens — while maintaining private units. Decisions made collectively. Costs shared. Isolation reduced.

**Key elements:**
- **Shared common spaces** — community kitchens, guest rooms, workshops, laundry, and gardens
- **Private living units** — each household maintains autonomy and personal space
- **Collective governance** — residents make decisions together through consensus or democratic process
- **Reduced costs** — shared resources mean lower per-household expenses

### Open-Source Architecture

Building plans, material specifications, and construction techniques shared freely. Anyone can adapt designs to local conditions and materials.

**Models that work:**
- **WikiHouse** — open-source building system designed for CNC cutting and community assembly
- **OpenStructures** — modular construction grid compatible across projects
- **Earthship designs** — off-grid buildings made from recycled materials
- **Local material techniques** — cob, rammed earth, bamboo, and other place-based methods

### Dignified Shelter for All

Housing-first approaches recognize that you can't solve any other problem while someone is unhoused.

- Tiny house villages for immediate shelter
- Adaptive reuse of empty commercial buildings
- Community-built emergency housing
- Supportive housing with wraparound services

## Getting Started

**As an individual:**
- Learn about housing cooperatives in your area
- Explore open-source building plans
- Support housing-first initiatives
- Join a tool-sharing library for home repair

**As a community:**
- Research starting a community land trust
- Organize a co-housing development
- Map vacant and underused properties
- Advocate for inclusionary zoning policies

**As a region:**
- Fund community land trust acquisition
- Reform zoning to allow diverse housing types
- Implement vacancy taxes on speculative holdings
- Support housing-first policies for homelessness

## Already Happening

This isn't theoretical. Around the world:
- Vienna's social housing model houses 60% of the city's population affordably
- Uruguay's FUCVAM cooperative movement has housed 30,000+ families through mutual aid construction
- WikiHouse provides open-source housing designs downloadable by anyone
- Finland virtually eliminated homelessness through housing-first policies
- Community land trusts in Burlington, Vermont have kept housing affordable for decades

The designs exist. The models work. What's needed is political will and community organization.
