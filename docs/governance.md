# Layer 3: Governance

## The Problem

Most governance systems were designed centuries ago for a world that no longer exists. They concentrate decision-making in distant capitals, create professional political classes, and reduce citizen participation to voting every few years for people you've never met.

The result: people feel powerless. Decisions that affect their daily lives are made by strangers. Lobbyists and corporations have more influence than communities. Trust in institutions collapses. And when people feel unheard, they either disengage or turn to authoritarian strongmen who promise simple answers.

## The Alternative

Governance that starts local, stays transparent, and gives people real power over the decisions that affect them.

### Subsidiarity

Every decision should be made at the smallest level where it can be made effectively.

**The principle:**
- Your street? Your neighbors decide.
- Your neighborhood? Your neighborhood council.
- Your city? City-level assembly.
- Regional or global issues? Federated councils with delegates from below.

No decision should be made at a higher level if it can be made effectively at a lower one. Power flows upward by delegation, not downward by command.

### Liquid Democracy

A hybrid between direct democracy and representative democracy.

**How it works:**
- You can vote directly on any issue you care about
- For issues you don't have time or expertise for, you delegate your vote to someone you trust
- You can delegate different topics to different people
- You can revoke your delegation at any time
- All votes and delegations are transparent and auditable

This means experts get more influence on technical topics, passionate people get more voice on issues they care about, and no one is stuck with a representative who ignores them.

### Rotating Stewardship

Leadership is service, not power. Positions rotate regularly to prevent entrenchment.

**Key features:**
- Term limits on all positions — short enough to prevent power accumulation
- No professional political class — service is temporary
- Mandatory rest periods between terms of service
- Leaders are accountable to the people who selected them and can be recalled

### Restorative Justice

When harm occurs, the goal is healing — not punishment.

**How it works:**
- Those who cause harm face the people they've harmed
- Together they work out how to repair the damage
- Community support for both parties
- Focus on understanding root causes and preventing recurrence
- Punishment only as a last resort when safety requires it

### Conflict Resolution

Disagreement is natural and healthy. The system needs good ways to handle it.

**Tools:**
- **Community mediation** — trained mediators help resolve disputes
- **Citizens' assemblies** — randomly selected groups deliberate on contentious issues
- **Consensus-building processes** — structured dialogue to find solutions everyone can live with
- **Transparent debate** — public forums where all perspectives are heard

## Getting Started

**As an individual:**
- Attend local government meetings — most decisions that affect you are made locally
- Learn facilitation and mediation skills
- Practice consensus decision-making in your own groups
- Question any system that concentrates power without accountability

**As a community:**
- Start a neighborhood assembly — regular meetings where everyone has a voice
- Create participatory budgeting — let residents decide how some public money is spent
- Establish community mediation services
- Experiment with liquid democracy tools for local decisions

**As a region:**
- Implement participatory budgeting at the city level
- Create citizens' assemblies for major policy decisions
- Establish transparency requirements for all public decision-making
- Support experiments in new governance models

## Already Happening

- Porto Alegre, Brazil pioneered participatory budgeting — now used in thousands of cities worldwide
- Ireland used citizens' assemblies to resolve deeply divisive issues like abortion and same-sex marriage
- Rojava (northeast Syria) runs on a system of nested local councils with rotating leadership
- Dozens of liquid democracy platforms are being tested in political parties and organizations globally
- Indigenous governance systems worldwide have practiced consensus-based decision-making for millennia

Better governance isn't about finding the right leaders. It's about building systems that don't require heroes.
